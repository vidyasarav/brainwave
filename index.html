What if every child understood their brain?

BRAINWAVE brings neuroscience into K–12 classrooms by empowering teachers with the knowledge, tools, and inspiration to shape how students understand their brains, learning, and mental health—especially in under-resourced communities where this knowledge is needed most.

§ MANIFESTO

The Science of Learning

We believe every teacher has the potential to be a catalyst for change. Through immersive workshops, hands-on activities, and exclusive tours of UCLA neuroscience labs, we equip teachers with the latest brain research — translated into tools they can use starting Monday morning.

LAUSD-Approved Professional Development JSPCC-approved workshops · Salary point credit for teachers

Teachers as Change Agents

Educators are the most powerful force in a child's scientific journey. We equip them with cutting-edge neuroscience knowledge, evidence-based pedagogy, and a community of forward-thinking peers — because when a teacher grows, every student in their classroom grows with them.

Equity at the Core

We prioritize under-resourced schools across Los Angeles where neuroscience education is rare but needed most. By emphasizing early exposure to brain science, we inspire students from all backgrounds to explore STEM fields and see themselves as future scientists.

Most Recent December 2025 · UCLA Campus

Brains in Rest: The Neuroscience of Sleep and Its Role in Learning, Behavior & Development

Our most recent workshop dove deep into the science of sleep — from circadian biology and sleep architecture to adolescent sleep patterns and sleep in neurodiverse learners. UCLA researchers including Drs. Christopher Colwell, Ketema Paul, Gina Poe, and Ceazar Nave led sessions exploring how the sleeping brain consolidates memory, regulates attention, and supports academic success. Teachers visited working neuroscience labs, handled real brain specimens, and collaborated to design sleep literacy mini-lessons for their own classrooms.

June 25, 2025 · UCLA

Brains in Rest: The Neuroscience of Sleep and Its Role in Learning, Behavior & Development

Teachers explored sleep stages, circadian rhythms, and how the brain consolidates memory overnight. Sessions covered real-world barriers like screens and stress, sleep challenges in diverse learners, and brain-based pedagogy. Teachers built brain models, analyzed case studies, and designed their own sleep literacy mini-lessons to take back to their classrooms.

Workshop Cohort 2025
September 25, 2024 · UCLA

Neuroscience of Learning & Memory

BRAINWAVE's inaugural workshop explored how the brain learns and remembers. UCLA researchers covered the brain's pathways for connection in the classroom, the neuroscience of memory consolidation, and why sleep is essential for learning. Teachers heard cutting-edge talks — including why naps improve brain power and what songbirds reveal about human memory — then toured active UCLA neuroscience research labs.

Our Founding Story
Neuroscience isn't just for researchers in labs. It belongs in every classroom, in every community.
Dr. Vidya Saravanapandian
Founder & Director, BRAINWAVE · IRACDA Postdoctoral Fellow, UCLA Golshani Lab

Born and raised in India, Vidya earned her PhD in Neuroscience from UCLA. Recognizing that early exposure to brain science was transforming her own sense of possibility, she created BRAINWAVE to ensure every K–12 teacher in Los Angeles had access to the same knowledge — regardless of their school's ZIP code.
